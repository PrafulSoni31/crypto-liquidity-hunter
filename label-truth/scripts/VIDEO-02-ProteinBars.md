---

### [SECTION 1: THE CRITERIA — 0:25-1:15]

"Before we start, here's how I'm scoring each bar:

Protein quality — is it whey, casein, soy, or some cheap filler?
Sugar content — anything over 8 grams is candy territory
Ingredient list — shorter and cleaner is better
Artificial additives — sucralose, acesulfame K, artificial colors
Fiber — real fiber or fake fiber like IMO (isomalto-oligosaccharides)?
Calorie efficiency — how many calories per gram of protein?

Each bar gets a score out of 10. Let's go."

---

### [THE RANKING — 1:15-13:00]

**#15 (WORST): Met-Rx Big 100**
"Starting with the worst. 410 calories, 28g protein, but also 30g sugar and nearly 40 ingredients. This is a meal replacement pretending to be a protein bar. The ingredient list reads like a chemistry textbook. Sugar alcohols, corn syrup, palm kernel oil... Score: 2/10"

**#14: Clif Bar (Chocolate Chip)**
"This might shock some people. Clif Bar markets itself as an energy bar for athletes. 250 calories, but only 10g of protein and 17g of sugar. Brown rice syrup — their first ingredient — is basically liquid sugar. This is a glorified oat cookie. Score: 3/10"

**#13: PowerBar**
"Old school. 23g protein but loaded with sugar alcohols — maltitol, which is famous for causing digestive issues. Also contains palm kernel oil and artificial flavors. Your grandpa's protein bar. Time to upgrade. Score: 3.5/10"

**#12: Pure Protein Bar**
"20g protein, only 2g sugar. Sounds great on paper. But flip it over — sucralose, acesulfame potassium, and a 30+ ingredient list. They replaced sugar with artificial sweeteners and called it healthy. Score: 4/10"

**#11: Kind Protein Bar**
"Kind has great marketing. 'Ingredients you can see and pronounce.' But their protein bar has 12g protein and 8g of added sugar. For a protein bar, that ratio is weak. You're paying for almonds with sugar glue. Score: 4.5/10"

**#10: Gatorade Protein Bar**
"20g protein but Gatorade's food science team went crazy — maltodextrin, sugar, fructose, palm oil. Three different types of sugar. This is a sports candy bar with protein sprinkled in. Score: 4.5/10"

**#9: Nature Valley Protein Bar**
"10g protein, but 12g sugar. Chicory root fiber makes the fiber count look impressive, but it's added fiber — your body doesn't process it the same way. It's Nature Valley's granola bar in a protein costume. Score: 5/10"

**#8: One Bar**
"20g protein, 1g sugar. Clean numbers. But the ingredients tell a different story — contains sucralose, palm kernel oil, and IMO fiber (which many experts argue isn't real fiber and acts more like sugar in your body). Decent but deceptive. Score: 5.5/10"

**#7: Think! Protein Bar**
"20g protein, 0g sugar. No artificial sweeteners either — they use sugar alcohols instead. The ingredient list is moderate. Main concern: soy protein isolate as the primary protein source, which is lower quality than whey. Solid budget option. Score: 6/10"

**#6: Quest Bar**
"The cult favorite. 21g protein, 1g sugar, high fiber. Uses soluble corn fiber instead of IMO — which is a genuine upgrade. But it still has sucralose and palm kernel oil. Overhyped but not bad. Score: 6.5/10"

**#5: Barebells**
"20g protein, no added sugar. Clean taste, great texture. Swedish brand that's blowing up. Downside: contains sucralose and palm oil. But the overall profile is significantly cleaner than most competitors. Score: 7/10"

**#4: GoMacro**
"Organic, plant-based, simple ingredients. 11g protein from brown rice and pea protein. Only 3g added sugar. You can actually read and understand every ingredient. Lower protein but highest ingredient quality in this list. Score: 7.5/10"

**#3: No Cow Bar**
"20g protein, 1g sugar, plant-based. Uses brown rice protein and pea protein. Allergen-free — no dairy, no soy, no gluten. High fiber from soluble corn fiber. One of the cleanest high-protein options. Score: 7.5/10"

**#2: RXBAR**
"12g protein from egg whites, dates, and nuts. The entire ingredient list is on the front of the package — 'No B.S.' And they actually deliver. 5-7 ingredients total. Downside: only 12g protein and higher in natural sugars from dates. But in terms of food quality? Elite. Score: 8/10"

**#1 (BEST): Mighty Bar (Whole Food Protein)**
"This might not be the most popular, but it's the most honest. Whole food ingredients — real chicken or beef, dried fruits, spices. 15g protein from actual meat. No protein powder, no sugar alcohols, no artificial anything. It's not a traditional protein bar, and that's exactly why it wins. Score: 8.5/10"
