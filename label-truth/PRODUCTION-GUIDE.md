# Label Truth — Production Guide

## Step-by-Step Video Production (Every Video)

### Step 1: Research (30-45 min)
1. Go to USDA FoodData Central: https://fdc.nal.usda.gov/
2. Search for the product → get full nutrition + ingredient data
3. Check FDA warning letters: https://www.fda.gov/inspections-compliance-enforcement-and-criminal-investigations/compliance-actions-and-activities/warning-letters
4. Google "[product name] lawsuit" or "[product] controversy"
5. Check EWG: https://www.ewg.org/foodscores/
6. Read 2-3 recent studies on key ingredients (Google Scholar)

### Step 2: Script Writing (30-45 min)
- Use this structure EVERY time:
  ```
  HOOK (0:00-0:30) — shocking fact or question
  FRONT LABEL (0:30-2:00) — what they want you to see
  INGREDIENTS (2:00-6:00) — decode each one
  HIDDEN TRUTH (6:00-8:00) — what they don't tell you
  VERDICT (8:00-9:00) — score + honest recommendation
  CTA (last 15 sec) — subscribe + comment prompt
  ```
- Keep sentences SHORT. One idea per sentence. Written for speech, not reading.
- Use AI (ChatGPT/DeepSeek) to draft, then fact-check and add personality.

### Step 3: Voiceover (10-15 min)
**ElevenLabs Setup:**
1. Go to https://elevenlabs.io/
2. Sign up free (10K characters/month = ~3-4 videos)
3. Pick voice: "Adam" (deep, authoritative) or "Josh" (warm, trustworthy)
4. Settings: Stability: 60%, Clarity: 75%, Style: 20%
5. Generate in chunks (paragraph by paragraph for better quality)
6. Download as MP3

**Free Alternative (unlimited):**
- Edge TTS via command line (sounds surprisingly good)
- `edge-tts --voice en-US-GuyNeural --text "script here" --write-media output.mp3`

### Step 4: Visuals (30 min)
**Get product images:**
- Official brand website (press/media section)
- Amazon product listing images
- Walmart.com product images
- Take your own photo of similar products if available locally

**Create graphics in Canva (free):**
- Ingredient breakdown slides
- Comparison charts
- Score/verdict cards
- Use brand colors (Dark Green #1B5E20 + Red #D32F2F + White)

**Stock footage (all free):**
- Pexels.com — grocery stores, people eating, food prep
- Pixabay.com — food b-roll
- Coverr.co — lifestyle footage

### Step 5: Edit in CapCut (45-60 min)
**Timeline structure:**
```
Audio track:     [Voiceover MP3]
Video track 1:   [Product images + slides]
Video track 2:   [Stock footage as b-roll]
Text track:      [Key words appearing on screen]
Music track:     [Background music at 15-20% volume]
```

**CapCut tips:**
- Use "Auto Captions" → American English → edit for accuracy
- Add zoom-in keyframes on ingredient lists when discussing them
- Use green/red color flashes for good/bad ingredient moments
- Transition: simple cuts or slight zooms. No flashy transitions.
- Export: 1080p, 30fps

### Step 6: Thumbnail in Canva (15 min)
**Template:**
```
[LEFT 40%]     Product image (high-res, slight angle)
[CENTER 40%]   Bold text (2-4 words MAX)
[RIGHT 20%]    Icon (checkmark / X / warning / question mark)
[BACKGROUND]   Dark gradient or contrasting color
```

**Rules:**
- Text must be readable on a phone screen
- Maximum 4 words on thumbnail
- Use RED for negative ("TOXIC?" "WORST") and GREEN for positive ("BEST" "#1")
- Product must be instantly recognizable
- Your face is NOT on it (faceless channel)

### Step 7: Upload & SEO (15 min)
1. Title: Include the product name + emotional trigger word
   - "What's REALLY inside [Product]?"
   - "I Ranked Every [Category] — #1 Will Shock You"
   - "[Product]: Healthy or SCAM?"
2. Description: First 2 lines are most important (show in search)
3. Tags: 15-20 tags mixing broad + specific
4. Thumbnail: Upload custom
5. Cards + End screen: Link to related videos
6. Publish time: 9:00 AM EST (6:30 PM IST) — US morning = peak
