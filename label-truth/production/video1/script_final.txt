[VIDEO #1: THE CHEERIOS TRUTH]

[0:00-0:30] HOOK:
Cheerios. America's favorite cereal. General Mills calls it "Heart Healthy," but I read the label. What I found doesn't match the marketing. Welcome to Label Truth. Let's break it down.

[0:30-2:00] THE CHOLESTEROL CLAIM:
The box says it lowers cholesterol. Technically true, but you'd need to eat three bowls a day to get enough soluble fiber to matter. That's a lot of carbs just for a "maybe" benefit.

[2:00-5:30] THE INGREDIENTS:
Whole Grain Oats? Excellent. 
Corn Starch? Just a filler. 
Sugar? Only 1 gram in Original, which is great, but Honey Nut is a sugar trap with 12 grams. That is like eating three Oreos for breakfast.
Verdict so far? Lenient. It is better than most, but watch the variety.

[5:30-7:30] THE HIDDEN TRUTH:
The labels don't mention Glyphosate. Independent tests found Roundup residue in many samples. While "legal," it is something General Mills doesn't put in their commercials. Plus, synthetic vitamins aren't the same as real food nutrients.

[7:30-9:00] FINAL VERDICT:
Our Label Truth Score: 6.8 out of 10. 
It is one of the cleanest big-box cereals, but don't let the "Heart Healthy" badge blind you to the processing and potential contaminants.
