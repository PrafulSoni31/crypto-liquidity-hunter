Now let's flip it over. Here's the full ingredient list for Original Cheerios:

Whole Grain Oats, Corn Starch, Sugar, Salt, Tripotassium Phosphate, Vitamin E — mixed tocopherols — added to preserve freshness.

Six ingredients. That's actually... not terrible. Let me break each one down.

Whole Grain Oats — the star. Actual whole grain. This is genuinely good. Oats contain beta-glucan, which does support heart health. No argument here. Point for Cheerios.

Corn Starch — this is a filler and binder. It helps hold the O shape. Not harmful, but adds zero nutrition. It's basically empty carbs.

Sugar — here's where it gets interesting. Original Cheerios has only 1 gram of sugar per serving. That's legitimately low. BUT — and this is important — Honey Nut Cheerios? 12 grams of sugar per serving. That's the same as eating three Oreo cookies for breakfast. And guess which one sells more? Honey Nut. By a LOT.

Salt — 140 milligrams of sodium per serving. About 6% of your daily value. Not alarming, but it adds up if you're having a big bowl.

Tripotassium Phosphate — sounds scary, right? It's actually a food-grade mineral salt used to balance acidity and help the cereal keep its texture in milk. The FDA considers it Generally Recognized As Safe — GRAS. No real concerns here unless you have kidney issues.

Vitamin E, also known as mixed tocopherols — used as a preservative. Also an antioxidant. This is actually fine. It prevents the oats from going rancid. Better than artificial preservatives like BHT.
